Underwater ROV Development

My MEng dissertation was carried out at Frontier Robotics, based in the National Robotarium, and focused on advancing underwater vehicle perception through sonar-based vision systems. The project aimed to design and implement reliable localisation and feature-matching techniques for subsea environments, where optical vision is often unreliable due to turbidity and poor visibility.

The core of the work involved developing and deploying a neural network-based sonar feature matching pipeline within ROS2. This included implementing the SONIC architecture, and integrating it with real-time data streams for online operation. Alongside this, I built a stereo camera calibration pipeline to enable accurate 3D reconstruction and ground-truth comparisons for underwater vision tasks.

To optimise performance, I explored and benchmarked various preprocessing methods, including anisotropic diffusion, non-local means filtering, and unsharp masking, to enhance sonar image clarity. With these improvements, the system achieved an operational rate of 2–3 Hz — fast enough for deployment on live ROV platforms. Extensive testing and validation ensured robustness of the algorithms, and the project demonstrated the potential for machine learning to unlock new levels of reliability in subsea robotic perception.

The dissertation was awarded a mark of 84% (First Class), and has since contributed to ongoing research in the lab exploring the next generation of perception systems for autonomous underwater vehicles.

Taking on the design and build of a fully custom underwater ROV was an exciting opportunity to push my technical limits and deliver something that directly supports the lab's ongoing research. This project aligned with my long-standing interest in marine robotics and offered a rare chance to work across the full stack - from mechanical systems and electronics to software and integration. The goal was clear: create a robust, modular, and capable platform that could serve as a long-term research workhorse. With a tight budget and timeline, we delivered a fully functional ROV with onboard cameras, a DVL, and a manipulator arm in under three months - proving not only our technical ability but also our commitment to building impactful tools for scientific exploration.
