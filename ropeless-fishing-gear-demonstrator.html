Ropeless Fishing Gear Demonstrator

Creel fishing still faces costly gear loss, theft, and the serious risk of whale entanglement from persistent surface lines. I contributed to the design of a ropeless alternative that removes those lines entirely while driving the price down to £600-£800 per unit - a fraction of the ~£8,000 typically charged for commercial systems.

At its core, the system uses an acoustic modem to send commands that a Raspberry Pi Pico W decodes onboard. That signal actuates a switchable magnet to release the buoy and line on demand, allowing the gear to be located and recovered without any surface marker left in the water during deployment. Around this release mechanism sits a practical, field-ready package: inductive charging, a custom Li-ion battery pack, custom PCBs, and a lightweight companion app for checks and setup. The housings and mounts were designed with CAD, 3D-printed, and integrated with off-the-shelf hardware to keep builds fast and costs low.

In a recent three-day sea trial, the unit was deployed continuously in seawater and released reliably. We also confirmed acoustic command and release from approximately 1,000 m (1 km). One deployment brought up 20 crabs,providing a useful real-world validation of both robustness and workflow.

For fishermen, the benefits are straightforward: removing surface lines reduces entanglement risk, while the low build cost and simple maintenance make scaling feasible. Installation is quick, servicing is minimal, and the hardware is engineered for tough, repetitive use offshore. The result is a credible, sustainable step toward modernising creel operations - one that balances commercial needs with better protection of marine life.

Feature Breakdown

Switchable Magnet Release

A remotely activated mechanism enabling precise control over fishing gear deployment.

Acoustic Signal Activation

Utilises high-frequency sound waves to communicate commands for gear release and retrieval.

Raspberry Pi Microcontroller

Serves as the brain of the operation, ensuring real-time processing and responsiveness.

Robust Design

Constructed to endure the challenging marine environment, ensuring durability and reliability.

User-Friendly Interface

Simplifies operation via either a bespoke mobile app, or an waterproof control box, making gear management efficient and accessible.

Deep Sleep Functionality

Conserves energy by entering a low-power state during periods of inactivity.
Since September 2023, I've worked on the Ropeless Fishing Gear. It sits squarely at the intersection of my interests - embedded robotics, communication, and building systems that actually work in the field - while advancing a sustainability goal I care about. This project has let me turn theory into deployed hardware: designing electronics and firmware, packaging the system for harsh marine conditions, and proving it at sea. It's been a sustained, hands-on effort with a clear purpose: deliver a low-cost robotic tool that protects marine life and is practical for working fishermen.
